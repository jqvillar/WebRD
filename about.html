I’m glad you came! I'm Janine Villareal, and I aspire to be a UX/UI Designer. I want to tell you a short story, to be more specific, mine. So, I’m a graduate from the University of Calgary with a concentration in Software Engineering. School was eye-opening but I felt that I had learned so much, yet I knew nothing. Feeling overwhelmed with the idea of having to, “adult” and facing the pressures of having to find a job quickly, I decided to leave and really take a step back and find out more about myself. What I love about life, What I enjoy doing, and what i'm passionate about. So I decided to travel.

I decided to go to London, France, and Germany and throughout my travels I started to realize that I’m passionate about people. Connecting with them, learning from them, hearing their stories, and helping them. Equipped with new experiences, I made a pact with myself to find a way to incorporate what I had learned in university with what I enjoyed and am passionate about. This eventually lead me to UX/UI design.

What I love about UX/UI Design

What I love most about UX/UI Design (especially UX Desgin) is the ability to spread enjoyment without directly being there and also connecting with them in a digital space. As someone who has worked many years in customer service, when someone tells you that their day hasn’t been the best but with your service you managed to help uplift their mood, you can’t help but feel a sense of accomplishment and satisfaction. I value and am passionate about learning and as a UX/UI Designer you’re able to connect with people and gain insight into how they think, through activities like user research and user interviews. They’re teaching you what they know, what their challenges are, and how they do things, just to name a few. Moreover, I also value creativity and being a UX/UI Designer I believe that you are given an opportunity to be creative, think outside of the norm in order to solve business problems, take part in value creation but also respresent the user and give them the best experience possible. Almost like being the bridge between the business and the users.

My Typical Work Day

Before the beginning of the week I always create a to-do list and listing items by priority. Then I plan my day and how i'm going to accomplish the things on my list. Some items on my list include reading, practice, research, networking, working on projects, just to name a few. (CREATE A MEANINGFUL LIST)

After creating a plan I motivate myself and envision what I want to achieve. I then work on my tasks, whether it's working on my web site, reading and taking notes, practicing JQuery, just to name a few.

I relax. After working, on average 5-6 hours(sometimes more depending on how much I need to get done) I make sure to take care of myself by relaxing and getting some me time. I do this by playing video games, watching something on YouTube or Netflix, or just taking a nice relaxing bath.
